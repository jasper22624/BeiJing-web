Summer Palace

The Summer Palace, located in Beijing, is a stunning imperial garden and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, renowned for its beautiful landscapes and rich history. Spanning over 700 acres, it features a mix of natural scenery and exquisite architecture, including pavilions, temples, bridges, and the picturesque Kunming Lake.

Originally constructed in the 18th century during the Qing Dynasty, the Summer Palace served as a royal retreat from the summer heat. Its design harmonizes with the surrounding hills and water, embodying traditional Chinese landscape aesthetics. Key attractions include the Longevity Hill, the Seventeen-Arch Bridge, and the Marble Boat.
